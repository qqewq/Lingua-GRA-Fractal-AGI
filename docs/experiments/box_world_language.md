## üá¨üáß 8. English summary (short)

The Box World Language experiment uses a simple grid environment with two agents:

- **Speaker**: sees the full world state and sends a discrete message.
- **Mover**: sees only local observations plus the message and must solve a box-related task (move/open/place objects).

The communication protocol emerges via reinforcement learning as in referential games and emergent communication literature,[web:120][web:123][web:127] but here we:

- embed messages through the semantic level of Lingua GRA;
- measure the **fractal dimension** (correlation dimension D‚ÇÇ) of the cloud of message embeddings over training;[web:38][web:34][web:105]  
- optionally add a fractal regularizer to steer the emergent language towards a target fractal profile inspired by natural languages.

By comparing D‚ÇÇ and qualitative structure of message embeddings across training phases and conditions (with/without fractal regularization), we can study how geometric properties of the emergent protocol relate to its compositionality, robustness, and similarity to human-like language structures.[web:38][web:45][web:105]
